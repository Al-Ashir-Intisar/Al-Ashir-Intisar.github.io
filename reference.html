Blease, C., Kaptchuk, T. J., Bernstein, M. H., Mandl, K. D., Halamka, J. D., & DesRoches, C. M. (2019). Artificial Intelligence and the Future of Primary Care: Exploratory Qualitative Study of UK General Practitionersâ€™ Views. (Journal of Medical Internet Research).

Main Focus: The integration of AI in primary care and general practitioners'(GP) perspectives on it. This study explores the perceptions and attitudes of GPs towards the integration of Artificial Intelligence into primary care. Through qualitative interviews, the researchers found that GPs are cautiously optimistic about the potential of AI to improve diagnostic accuracy and efficiency in administrative tasks. However, there is significant concern regarding the ethical implications, such as patient data privacy and the potential for AI to depersonalize the patient-care provider relationship. The study highlights a mixed reception among GPs, with many emphasizing the need for AI to complement rather than replace human judgment in healthcare.

Luxton, D. D. (2014). Artificial Intelligence in Psychological Practice: Current and Future Applications and Implications. (Journal: Professional Psychology: Research and Practice) Main Focus: The use of AI in psychological practice and the ethical implications thereof.

This article examines the current and potential future applications of AI in psychological practice. The paper discusses various AI applications, including automated psychological assessment tools, therapeutic chatbots, and AI in clinical decision support systems. Luxton highlights critical ethical issues such as ensuring confidentiality, obtaining informed consent when AI tools are used, and the risk of AI making autonomous decisions without sufficient human oversight. The article calls for the development of ethical guidelines and training for psychologists to handle AI technologies responsibly, ensuring that these innovations benefit patient care without compromising professional standards.
